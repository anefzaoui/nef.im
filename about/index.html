This is Ahmed. Hiya o/

My name is Ahmed Nefzaoui (hence nef.im) – a front-end developer from Tunisia (UTC+1). I have a Bachelor’s Degree in Computer Sciences, and I work with companies around the world.

Over the course of my still-young career, I worked all of my previous jobs as a remote employee, of course I had to travel a lot, but also giving me an excellent opportunity to work with different kinds of people. And in CRAZY timezones.

I started off in 2014 as a WordPress Front-End web developer in Tunisia where I had the task of RTL-ing our clients’ theme UIs so they can run them on Arabic, Hebrew and Urdu instances of WordPress. So my work involved CSS, JavaScript, HTML5 and PHP.

The main milestone that enhanced my Front-End development skills, and the one I’m mostly proud of is the years of contribution to Mozilla, followed by recruitment in Mozilla partner companies (specifically Phoxygen Ltd). Where my job was to work on the Front-End of Firefox OS, the mobile operating system built on web technologies (HTML5, CSS3 and JavaScript). after that me and an awesome equipe got an assignment of delivering a version of Firefox OS (specifically v2.0) that is fully compatible with the Middle East and North Africa market requirements, in order for our partner – Orange – to launch the product in that area. I now master CSS3 and am averaging in JavaScript: es5, es6, while trying my best to stay up to date with all the new specs being implemented in major browsers.

Then I moved to Mozilla Corp for a short term contract as part of the Developer Relations team, where I was writing technical articles to help the community better understand obscure development topics such as RTL.

I also master git and using the Linux terminal in general, which were always required in my previous jobs.

My last position was a contracting job for a Technical Evangelist / Community Manager for a company found by former Mozilla CTO Andreas Gal where I had to build and maintain a community, and evangelize for the technology it was developing.
